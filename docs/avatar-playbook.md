# Avatar Playbook — Pay-Per-Call Agency
## PRISM + 18 Questions + 5 Questions + PMPS Framework

**Purpose:** This document is the living foundation for all cold email, ad copy, and social posts.
Every entry is built from the Arnav Srivastv "Unlimited Clients" framework applied to each vertical
in the lead finder dashboard. The dashboard's PRISM research (Google rank, Yelp gap, contacts, news,
AI analysis) provides the per-lead personalization that gets inserted via ReachInbox variables.

**ReachInbox Variables Available Per Lead:**
`{{firstName}}` `{{lastName}}` `{{companyName}}` `{{city}}` `{{phone}}` `{{website}}`
`{{googleRank}}` `{{googleRating}}` `{{totalRatings}}` `{{yelpRating}}` `{{yelpReviewCount}}`
`{{yelpGap}}` `{{yelpUrl}}` `{{businessType}}` `{{coldEmailAngle}}` `{{businessCategory}}`
`{{businessSummary}}` `{{customerType}}` `{{recentNewsTitle}}` `{{recentNewsSnippet}}`

**PMPS Structure (every email/post):**
> Problem/Pain → Mistakes → Pleasures → Solution

**5 Pre-Write Questions (answer before every email/post):**
1. What effect do you want to produce in their mind?
2. What feeling do you want to produce in their mind?
3. What would they want to know first?
4. What creates urgent desire in them right away?
5. What is the MAIN PICTURE you want to draw in their mind?

## CATEGORY 1 — HOME SERVICES TRADES

*Shared pain across all trades: inconsistent call volume, slow seasons, competing with large
franchises (HomeAdvisor, Angi, Thumbtack) that sell their contact info to 5 competitors,
Yelp filtering their reviews, Google ranking them invisible.*

---

### Plumber

**Avatar:** Owner-operator or small crew, 1–10 employees. Handles emergencies (burst pipes,
water heater failures) and scheduled jobs (installations, remodels). Most revenue comes from
emergency calls they can't predict or control.

**Deepest Desire:** A steady, predictable flow of booked jobs every single day — not feast or famine.

**Biggest Problem:** Their phone only rings when there's an emergency — they can't control timing
or volume. Slow weeks mean they're paying labor with no income coming in.

**Biggest Frustration:** Paying Angi/HomeAdvisor $40–80/lead only to compete against 4 other
plumbers who got the same name at the same time. The customer calls whoever answers first.

**What Keeps Them Up at Night:** A slow week where they're paying 2–3 guys and the phone isn't ringing.

**Biggest Mistake:** Relying on word-of-mouth and Yelp, while their Yelp reviews are being filtered
({{googleRating}} on Google vs {{yelpRating}} on Yelp — {{yelpGap}} stars suppressed).

**What They Complain About:** "I paid $600 this month to Angi and got one job that barely covered it."

**State of Awareness:** Sophisticated — they've tried everything and are cynical. Lead with proof, not promises.

**Urgency Level:** HIGH — every day without calls is money lost.

**5 Pre-Write Questions:**
1. Effect: Make them realize their current spend is guaranteed waste vs pay-per-call which is guaranteed ROI
2. Feeling: Frustrated at current vendors, cautiously hopeful
3. Want to know first: That I only charge when a real person calls — no upfront cost risk
4. Urgent desire: Their phone ringing TODAY with a booked job
5. Main picture: A notification on their phone — "New call: Water heater emergency, Clearwater"

**PMPS Cold Email:**
```
Subject: Paying for leads that go to 4 other plumbers too?

Hi {{firstName}},

PAIN: {{companyName}} is ranking #{{googleRank}} on Google Maps for plumbers in {{city}}.
Every homeowner with a burst pipe at midnight searches Google and calls the top 3 results.
Right now, most of those calls aren't going to you.

MISTAKE: Most plumbers in {{city}} are paying Angi or HomeAdvisor $40–80 per lead —
then racing 4 other plumbers to answer the phone first. You're paying for the privilege
of competing. And Yelp is quietly hiding your real reputation: you're at {{googleRating}}
on Google but {{yelpRating}} on Yelp because they filter reviews from customers who
don't write reviews often.

PLEASURE: Imagine waking up to 3–5 inbound calls from homeowners who dialed a number
looking specifically for a plumber in {{city}}, connected directly to YOUR phone.
No competition. No bidding war. Just a real person who needs a plumber right now.

SOLUTION: That's pay-per-call. I set up a campaign targeting plumbing emergencies in
{{city}} and route every call exclusively to you. You only pay when the call lasts
over 90 seconds — a real conversation. No monthly retainer to start.

Worth a quick call this week?
```

## NOTES & UPDATES

*This document is the living foundation. Add notes here as you test and learn what works.*

- Yelp gap angle is strongest opener for any vertical where the gap is 0.5+ stars
- Google rank #1–5: use the "you're close but missing calls" angle
- Google rank #6–20: use the "invisible to searchers" angle
- Legal and insurance verticals: lead with data and specifics, not emotion — they're sophisticated buyers
- Emergency categories (water damage, locksmith, towing, garage door): urgency is everything, skip the warm-up
- Recurring revenue verticals (pest control, landscaping, pool): pitch the contract, not the one-time job
